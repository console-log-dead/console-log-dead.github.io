Sustainability in SP

As Singapore's first polytechnic, Singapore Polytechnic has always been thinking about the environment and has taken steps to ensure its campus has as little effects on the natural environment by building around the nature that has existed here.

Singapore Polytechnic believes that in order to be sustainable and safeguard the wellbeing of the staff and students in SP, there is the need to create safe & green spaces. To be more sustainable, SP has worked on its practices, curricula, partnerships and people to make sure that a sustainable future can be reached by everyone.

Here are some examples on how SP has ensured sustainability in its campus.

Eco-Friendly Buildings

SP has strived to make every building reach the BCA Green Mark Platinum certification by 2025. Currently, 4 buildings are certified as such - T3B, T18A, T18B and Sports Arena. The BCA Green Mark certification is a rating system by the Building and Construction Authority to evaluate whether a building is sustanably designed and run.
Photo by me.

Building Around Nature

SP's campus has been planned in such a way that the existing nature could be intergrated with the buildings in the campus. This has led to SP having a wide biodiversity of 230 different types of plants, including Brown Woolly Fig Heritage Trees found near Food Court 1.
Photo by Singapore Polytechnic

Reducing Plastic Waste

SP has installed more than 110 water coolers and stopped giving out plastic bottles at events so as to encourage students and staff to use water dispensers around campus and encourage them to develop eco-conscious habits.
